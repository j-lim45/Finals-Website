Broken tramway inside campus fixed

Author: Quirrel | Written: Aug 21 2024

The broken tramway inside the UH campus has been fixed according to university engineers, and will resume operations next month.

After years of inactivity, the "Failed Tramway" of the university has been repaired. Head Engineer Menderbug stated in a press briefing that the transportation service rehabilitation project has officially ended, as it had its test run a week prior. Students of the campus can now make use of the tram once again to traverse through the campus, using a tram pass. Every tram pass that was availed before the suspension of sale will still be eligible for usage, and students who wish to avail for the tram pass can do so at the nearest UH store.

Much to the dismay of stag drivers, the Association of Stag Transport (AST) has staged a protest at the entrance of the campus as they compete with another mode of transport. Stags have been the only way of transport for centuries, and with the comeback of the tramway, stags have been feared to be phasing out soon as more efficient mode of transports are introduced.
